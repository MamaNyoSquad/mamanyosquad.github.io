Admirant of the Past

Fiona’s Inside Story

Published 20 September 2021

Browsing through the biography book were some of her interesting experiences at the island, including her deep affection, revealing her nature of being an admirer. Admitting that it was the previous Owner whom she admired, how could she handle the stresses of being with him back to her faraway home?


This part of the Inside Story takes place months before the previous Owner’s disappearance, the Prologue of Venus Island Diaries - Main Story.

POV: Fiona

It’s already past 3-o’clock in the afternoon.

As I look inside the wardrobe which contains a collection of outfits that I took before my arrival on this island, I notice a light-purple gown which I used to wear to take photos of myself back at the palace. It looks different from the outfit I wore when I stepped in to meet him in person, in terms of the design.

It has a Tanzanite on both sides, sprinkled with a bit of Aquamarine and Lavender Quartz, and a translucent veil helming the skirt. Attached to it is a picture of my family--The King and Queen.

As soon as I was about to pull the gown out, I heard a knock on the door. “Please come in.”

A tall, tender, pink-haired girl approaches behind me. It was Elise, a good friend of mine for a good few months following her arrival to this island. “That sure looks fantastic,” she said, admiring the gown I just pulled out of.

“Thanks,” I compliment, smiling as I turn to face her beauty. “To be honest, I don’t know which one to wear for tonight.”

“You can actually wear what you think suits you, but I gotta be honest here: That one looks pretty good,” she said, trying to take a closer look at the outfit I have in my hand. “When did you have it, by any chance?”

“A long time ago, I’m afraid.”

She glisters in awe as I say that sentence. “Please tell me more about it,” she begs.

“Alright... I mainly used this one for photoshoots back home at a faraway place,” I said, tending to the gown. “That was before I got the news about Ona-sama a few days later. The next thing I knew, I had already sent him a bunch of pictures because I wanted him to like it.”

“Wow... I didn’t expect you to be his admirer after all.”

“That’s why I came here in the first place... I want to make a good impression to sway him and make him feel that I love him very much... and be his perfect bride,” I smile.

“That’s pretty romantic, if I should say so myself,” Elise responds, a little moved by my responses.

“To be honest, I haven’t worn this for him just yet. I probably might, because I got some visitors coming.”

“Really? Who were they?”

I wink sweetfully. “You’ll see what I mean tonight. Now please excuse me for a bit as I try to wear this one.”

That evening...

“Ona-sama!!!” I called up to him who was looking at the ocean. I approach slowly, in the gown I haven’t shown to him yet. You know, the light-purple one. “How do I look?”

“You’re... beautiful,” he expresses his awe, holding my hands. Elise approaches us in her casual outfit. He’s also wearing his casual one.

“She wanted to show you her gown for a long time. I’m not sure if you haven’t heard of it,” she spills the beans, making me slightly upset.

“Elise!!!”

He chuckles, a smile on his face. “It’s alright. I actually didn’t see that coming, to be honest.”

“Really?”

“Yeah,” he releases my hands, looking at my pristine figure.

“Oh right, we got some visitors from home. They’ll be right here in a few minutes,” I informed immediately, catching Elise’s attention.

“You got it,” Elise said, then left the area to prepare the clipboard.

“I guess she’s mentally prepared for this thing.”

“Of course,” I chuckle. “She’s been into training since she arrived. Call her a tour guide or a waitress, everything goes.”

“Wow.”

She immediately comes back, a few things in her hand. On her left is a clipboard and a pen, while on her right is a bunch of greeting cards. Oh wait, when was the last time we had an arts and crafts project of that equivalent?

He takes the greeting cards, pulling his own pen out of his pocket. Looking at me, he said in a lighter tone: “Let’s get to work. It should buy us time before they arrive on time.”

“Wait, what should we do with the greeting cards?” I ask.

“Let’s put the names of your family on each of these,” he suggests. “I’m sure they’ll like these as much as you do.”

I nod immediately, tending to some of the cards while Elise is waiting for the ship to arrive. That begs a question: Did they bring my pet dog with them?

Minutes later...

We’re almost done with the enumeration when we heard the ship’s siren. It’s one of the ships that’s named after me: The S.S. Royal Fiona III. Elise instructed the ship to direct to the pier, where we’ll be waiting for it to arrive.

At the pier, Misaki was waiting patiently for the visitors, flower necklaces in her hand. “I can’t believe the Royals will be here for the Princess,” she thought, immediately pausing to see us coming towards her direction. “Wait, Fiona’s the Princess?!”

“Misaki-sama,” I approached her gracefully, revealing the gown. “How do I look?”

“That’s pretty good,” she exclaims, turning to the left to see the ship about to dock. The girls begin to appear from all directions just to see the ship as it opens the entryway door.

The first to come out is my pet dog, John. As soon as he jumps to reach for my arms, other personalities start to get out of the ship, with Misaki greeting them one by one and Ona-sama giving them each a greeting card.

The butler is the next one to come out, approaching my direction to take a look at my gown. “You’ve done pretty well. This gown really suits you better.”

“Thank you,” I compliment with a smile on my face.

Just in time for the King and Queen to come out, in their most regal outfits possible. As Misaki tends to their flower necklaces, they turn their attention to Ona-sama who’s about to give them greeting cards. “So you’re the one our daughter was talking about,” he said, beginning the conversation.

“Yep,” Ona-sama replies, giving the last greeting card to the Queen.

“I can’t imagine how you would handle her briefly during her stay here. She must be very grateful to have you on her side,” the Queen said, patting his right shoulder as she turned to face me.

“Let’s get inside, then. The food is waiting to be served to our dear guests,” Misaki reprimands, leading the way inside the mansion. Ona-sama and I follow, holding hands as the butler tends to my dog. The others follow suit. Luckily, the girls were in their own Champs Elysees before they greeted the visitors. I’m not quite sure why.

Inside, various veggies and meat buffets are ready for everyone to enjoy dinner. Nagisa prepares the last of the dishes, seeing Ona-sama with a rather comfortable look. She’s actually our cook for this week, so that’s why she had a lot of dinner ideas.

One of them is the world-famous pork adobo. Believe it or not, we have prepared a Filipino cuisine for the first time in our lives. How quaint.

As everyone sits down except me, Ona-sama, the King and Queen, and Nagisa; I formally begin the dinner with a prayer. The King follows suit with a formal introduction, with the Queen telling how much excitement they had in visiting this island for the first time. Now it’s my turn to speak for the guests, not as a resident of the island... but as Ona-sama’s fiancé.

You heard it right. I stayed here just so that I can participate in festivals and train to be his perfect bride.

As words came out of my mouth, everyone clapped gracefully as they began to notice their new King in line. My father just told everyone that when I became the new Queen, I’ll be married to my future King, hence, he pointed to my relationship with Ona-sama. I turned back to see Nagisa in shock.

And then I remember something that she promised a few weeks ago, that she needs to stay close to him for the whole month.

I whisper in his ear, “Hey, about Nagisa...”

He nods, hopefully that he knows what to do. As soon as he instructs everyone to enjoy dinner, he then asks Nagisa and I to head outside to talk about things.

Nagisa immediately hugs him after we head to the café. “Please don’t leave me,” she begs.

I slowly lean forward and pat her head. He tries to calm her down, holding her hands as firmly as he can.

“I know it’s hard for me to admit what I feel, but...”

“I know,” Nagisa interrupts my statement. “But what about me? I liked him too.”

Gently caressing her back, Ona-sama responds, “I know...”

With no words left to mutter in each of us three, all we can do is just hug it out until she has been calmed down for the rest of the night.

The next morning...

I finally wake up to find myself in the Suite together with Ona-sama and Nagisa at the same time. She appears happy at this point, although I’m not sure how long that’ll last.

Nevertheless, I’m the first to get up, making Ona-sama his own coffee and Nagisa her own Strawberry Shake. Returning to the Suite to give them the delicacies, they’re already up, looking for me.

“Good morning,” I immediately said to them upon entering.

“Good morning to you, too,” Ona-sama and Nagisa both said back, with a smile on their faces.

“How about you? You don’t want breakfast?” He asks me, realizing that I haven’t made my own yet. Oops.

Placing the tray on the table, I hurriedly rush back to the kitchen to make myself some Herbal Tea. Luckily, I made it back to the Suite in time just to see them about to drink their blends. “Sorry about that...”

“No problem,” Nagisa smiles, patting on the cushion. “Now come join in.”

As I sit with my tea in hand, Ona-sama asks me directly, “How’s the family? Have they been doing well?”

I nod. “Yeah... It’s their first time being here, so I guess they’ll be alright.”

“I can see that,” Nagisa sips her shake. “But, what do you think they’ll be doing next?”

“I don’t know,” I sip. I didn’t even realize that they are still sleeping in the guest room, that I don’t know what to do next.

As he sips in his coffee, he pulls out a clipboard which lists what might be doing next. “Let’s see...”

The next hour...

I’m in my Sweet Chocolatier as I walk around the bay, wondering what they were doing as of late. I saw the Queen by the pier, looking at the ocean.

As I approach her by the right, she turns to smile at me. “So, has everything been good?”

“Yes, your Majesty.”

“I see,” the Queen turns back to gazing at the ocean. “I can remember one moment when my husband treated me to somewhere like this. It just feels mesmerizing for some reason.”

I stood over by her side, also watching the ocean as they went. “Can you please tell me about it?”

She nods, holding my hand. “You see, it was my first time when he took me out to a private island full of tranquility and memories. We had a lot of fun and quality time together, and that became a perfect spot for our dates, to be precise about it. We carried over the memories of staying there back at the palace.”

“At least you don’t get bored while doing so.”

“That’s right. Hopefully that happened to you during your stay here.”

“It was, and I’m grateful to be here. Not just because I enjoyed the amenities and everything this island has to offer, I feel more grateful that I met him... the one whom I chose to be with for the rest of my life... my precious Ona-sama.”

“I can see that,” she smiles at me.

From afar, I can see Ona-sama tending to some of the girls, while Nagisa is still clinging onto his arm. “I guess the others feel the same way I do,” I said.

It didn’t take quite long for the butler to approach us two, together with my pet dog John. “How is he doing?” I ask.

“Oh, he’s doing just fine,” he said, tending to the dog. “He’s having his time here without causing much trouble.”

“Pretty good,” the Queen smiles in delight. “Now, what should we do next?”

“I don’t know,” I said, looking back at the activities by the bay, just in time for Ona-sama to approach in my direction with a box in his hands.

As he hands the box to the Queen, I let out a curious look. What could be in that box?

“Should we open it around the block?” I ask, asking to carry the box.

“Sure,” the Queen nods. “It’s for us anyway.”

As we head over to the café, I immediately open the box to find a couple of things: A photobook, a bunch of letters, two rings, and a regal outfit?

Immediately pulling the outfit from the box, I showed it to him with a confused look on my face. “What’s this?”

“I, uh... I actually wanted to sew something like this for a long time. I hope you like it,” he looks away, a little flushed.

“Really?”

He nods slowly.

I glance in awe. “I can’t wait to wear this!”

The Queen glances at me as I run to the mansion to try the outfit. “She surely is excited to put it on,” she told Ona-sama.

“I guess, yeah,” he answers, a little relieved for a bit.

A few minutes trying to put on the outfit later...

I came back to see the Queen and Ona-sama chatting about what’s in the photobook. “Your Majesty? Ona-sama? How do I look?” I said, approaching their direction.

“You’re very splendid,” the Queen gives her remarks with a smile on her face. She looks at Ona-sama, asking about him.

“Oh, magnificent. You’re actually fitting for a literal Princess, as far as I’m concerned,” he said, tending to the outfit.

By appearance, it’s a light-purple dress adorned with white truffles. Attached to the waistline are a bunch of flower petals which appear to be in a solution that will not make them wilt no matter what. (It’s strange, I know.) At the bottom of the skirt, sprinkles of pristine crystals--which they call V-Stones--can be seen. The veil on the back is well-made, adding a touch of translucent silk and purple threads on the sides. The same threads were used for the letter "F" which is at the bottom of the veil. I shrouded myself with a mixture of Jasmine and Aloe to make it fragrant, like the usual.

“You actually forgot something,” he said, picking up something from the box. “Now if you may close your eyes for a bit...”

I did what was told, as I felt something brushing through my hair. “Now you may open your eyes,” he said.

It was... a tiara?

“I actually want to do this ahead of time because you’ll be leaving tonight, I guess.”

“Wait, we’re leaving tonight?” I ask in surprise.

“You and your family,” he said, looking at the Queen. “I just told her that I don’t want to join you because of responsibilities that I must do here. I hope you can understand.”

As soon as she said that, tears began running down my face. “But... I can’t just leave you behind...”

Immediately tackling him into an embrace, I soon began to cry. “No... I can’t leave you! I don’t wanna leave!”

“B- but-”

“No!” I interrupt the Queen’s attempt to speak. “I’ll stay with him, whatever it takes. Even if it means I have to disobey the Crown.”

Slowly, he pats me on my back, telling me to calm down. “There, there. Look, I’ll promise to send you letters day and night during your stay there. I’ll even ask for the Queen’s blessing to install a telephone line at the palace so that I can call you every night.”

“T- telephone line?” The Queen asks in shock.

“Don’t worry. Fiona knows what to do,” he reassures, patting my head as I continue to let my tears out my eyes in dismay that I might not see him again as soon as I return to the island.

That evening... The ship sounds its siren, the whole family is inside. I’m about to board when Misaki runs into me for a final embrace... as mutual sisters.

“I know things will be very hard for you when I’m gone, but I’ll promise to come back here as soon as I’m able.”

“I know,” she cries. “But what about Ona?”

As soon as she said that, Ona-sama approached our direction, handing me an envelope. “Please read it before you go.”

I immediately pull from Misaki’s embrace, taking the envelope. Inside is a letter of appreciation which reads as follows:

Fiona,

I hope you’re safe upon returning to the palace. It’s been a ride ever since your first arrival here as a Venus, and you’re getting better as time goes by. You also helped me become the best I am, and others become what they are today.

I can’t help but feel appreciated because of your endless dedication to serve everyone better, and feel thankful for what you do to keep everyone happy.

I also want to say that I appreciate your commitment to make everyone worth your while, and confess your feelings as time goes by. I will always remember what you do to make us happy.

I just hope that you’ll return soon so that we can continue to connect our hearts for the better good. May I hope to be in your care soon.

Love,
Your Ona-sama

While I was reading the note, tears came falling down my eyes. I feel more grateful that he wrote this for me, that I almost don’t want to leave. Ona-sama slowly pulls me into a serene embrace for the whole five minutes, telling him that I’m forever grateful and in his eternal debt as I smile softly in his arms.

We pulled ourselves from the embrace after such minutes, him wiping my tears with his cloth. “I guess I’ll see you soon,” I said.

“You’re welcome to come back anytime,” he nods, a smile on his face. “And, uh... I’ll get started with the letters tomorrow morning.”

“Please do...”

The ship sounds its final siren, prompting me to get aboard. From that, I just realized.

“Uh... Ona-sama?” I approach him again as soon as he’s about to leave. “There’s actually something that I must give to you before I go.”

“Hm?” He wonders about, curious on what it was.

I pulled something out from my pocket, hiding between my palm. “May I lend your hand, please?”

As soon as he does so, I distinctively hover my hand, dropping an object on his palm. Holding him for the final time, clenching to a fist, I slowly muster, “I’ll promise to return here as soon as I’m able to. And when I do, please don’t forget this gift. It’s a sign of our everlasting love. Please keep it in a safe place.”

What I just gave to him is none other than a ring. A special ring, capable of binding a true relationship between me and Ona-sama. As \soon as he wears it on my finger when I return, I’ll forever be bound to him for the rest of my life. In short, I’ll be the most beautiful bride on the Venus Island.

As I head inside the ship, I quickly get to the balcony to wave everyone goodbyes. I know that I’ll be leaving the island which is my second home, so I’ll promise myself to do whatever it takes to come back there and stay... for good.

The girls are already heading inside the mansion, but my gaze is still drawn to Ona-sama for some reason. He can’t leave his post until he can’t see the ship anymore. Oh, I just wished I didn’t become a Princess so that I could stay with him...

A few days later, back at the palace...

I’ve been returning to my duties as a Princess after I left the precious island. Tending to the paperwork and to the citizens of the kingdom, it felt like a fresh start.

But compared to before I entered the island for the first time, I felt... alone. Sure, I have my family here who can cope with my emotions, but it just feels like it. Without my dearest Prince, my Ona-sama... I just feel... helpless.

Back at the palace, I found some letters addressed from the island. While some of them were from the girls, the majority of them were from him. Immediately taking the envelopes, I head to my room to try and read them one by one. Trying to respond to them will take me the course of the night, as the package consists of twenty-five letters per day, the highest being at forty-two.

Oh, and the telephone line? We don’t have a dedicated service provider to begin with, so it’s not possible to install a telephone line that way, unfortunately. I just wrote the final letter to Ona-sama addressing that and other details that he covered.

Days gone by, and I’m off to the same routines. Only this time, we’re preparing because next week is my birthday.

Do you know that my birthday is on 11 February? No?

Anyways, I just sent a bunch of invitational cards to the residents, but I just can’t hamper this feeling. I couldn’t help but feel like I wanted to bring him into the fun, too. I decided to write a letter about it, and attached an invitation to my birthday party, including the scheduled arrival and departure of the ship that I chose (which for some reason was the ship that was docked at the island during the royal visit), inside the envelope. Oh, and I already had the blessing to use the ship for this occasion, so there’s nothing to worry about.

Fifteen hours before Fiona’s birthday...

Tonight is a long evening at the palace. I head outside to think of what to do when he’s here at the party. Do I have to tell him that I’m finally ready to confess about being his bride?

I was about to think deeply about it when the courier arrived. “A letter for the Princess,” he said, taking an envelope out of the box.

“Thanks for your hard work,” I smile, taking the envelope. “Make sure to sleep well.”

“Likewise, your Majesty.”

As he left the area, I immediately opened the envelope to reveal a strange flower petal along with a note. It’s one of the flowers that only bloom during the eve of a girl’s birthday, the note says.

“Wow...” I glance in awe.

From afar, the ship sounded its siren, preparing for departure as indicated. One guard approaches my direction: “The ship is ready to depart, madame.”

“Very well,” I said. “Please welcome him if he gets here.”

“Who was it that you’re speaking of?”

“My dearest Prince... my Ona-sama.”

“Very well.” He said, bowing his head as he left the area. Checking on the petal, it appears to have a stain of white shrouded about in gradient colors of purple and pink. It has a distinct fragrant smell, reminding me of the perfume I used before visiting the island for the first time.

I look up and smile at the glowing moonlight, thinking of the experiences I had with him during my stay. I can’t wait to tell him how grateful I am to be his admirer after all. All the sweet memories that we shared together... Ah, it’s finally a dream come true!

As I headed to my bedroom, a thought came to mind. “What if after the birthday party, I’ll treat him to a royal date?”

I smile as I close my eyes, looking at the shimmering stars across the balcony. I can’t believe I’m going to see him tomorrow! I must present myself in the most elegant dress that he’ll be interested in staying for long.

But... How about his responsibilities at the island? Surely he can’t just leave them behind because of the party!

Oh... What should I do?

The next morning (five hours before Fiona’s birthday)...

“A package for the Princess,” the courier said, entering the hallway.

“Thank you,” I smiled, taking the package. “Any word on the arrival yet?”

“Your ship, the S.S. Royal Fiona III, is due to arrive in the next two hours. I got word from the maritime that they’re expecting someone from an island off of the Japanese Pacific.”

“Thank you. I should get prepared for the party, then.”

“Very well. Best wishes on your birthday, your Majesty.”

“Thank you,” I bow, carrying the package as I head back to my room. Opening the package reveals a camera, some films, and another note that reads:

Here are the necessities we need to capture the best memories that we’ll be having while at the party. Make sure we utilize it well.

As soon as I take the stuff out of the box, I put on my bath towel to do my hour-long bath in preparation for the party.

Approximately two hours later...

About to finish my endearing hairstyle, I could hear the ship’s siren from afar. It’s returning to dock at the nearest pier! I immediately get off the chair to see the ship making a turn with an excitement on my face.

“Please, don’t get too excited. Your hairstyle-”

“I know! I know,” I slump back to my seat. “I need to look very elegant for him, you know.”

“Really?” The handmaiden asks. “Everyone was talking about your relationship with him three days ago.”

“They do?” I glance in shock. “W- would that be a problem during the party?”

“I don’t think that’s gonna be an issue, though. It seems everyone was really up to supporting your relationship endeavor.”

“I see,” I sigh in relief. “Let’s get this done.”

Upon finishing the hairstyle, make-up and everything, I quickly set off to the pier to see everyone gathering along there. With guards in front of me, they ordered the spectators to get out of the way.

Upon proceeding through I could only see the ship... and Lobelia, my childhood friend. “Where is he?” I ask.

“He’s... not gonna be here,” she said with a frown on her face.

“You’ve got to be kidding me... I did prepare in advance for his arrival, then you said he’s not gonna be here for the party?!”

“Y- you have to understand! I-”

“I will NOT! We will not begin this party until he’s here, OR at the palace!” I scream, walking away from the crowd as fast as I can.

How could he do this to me? He promised! Did he just make a fool out of me by luring my feelings into the brim?

“Get out of my way,” I ordered when somebody patted me on the shoulder, not daring to look behind.

“Hey!” The stranger called, stopping me in my tracks. Immediately turning my back, I can see a hooded man, handing me something.

“Take it,” he said. Slowly but surely, I took his note. He nods slowly, walking away through the crowd. Oh wait, that voice... sounds familiar.

I slowly open the note, that reads:

Meet me at the palace. You know who this is.

Right when I finished reading, trumpets can be heard from the watchtowers of the palace. A guard prompted me to get inside immediately.

Upon returning to the palace, I could only see the black hood lying on the floor. Picking it up, my heart started throbbing in fright. “Who is he?” I thought to myself.

“Guess who?” A voice echoes the hallway.

For some reason, it made me feel scared. “Who is it?”

“A friend of yours,” the voice replies. It’s making me feel more scared than before.

“Please tell me who you are!!!”

Silence ensued in the hallway for a few seconds that I was just about to cry in dismay in finding out that I might never see him at the party. I just couldn’t help but feel hopeless about this. Everything I worked for for the past week...

“Go to the ballroom, and scream his name.”

Determined, I nodded and ran to the ballroom just in time for the bells to start chiming. Preparing my breath and my voice, I scream on top of my lungs as loud as I can:

“Ona-sama!!!”

The birds started flying, the waves were crashing, the voices across the kingdom came to a halt. I slump on the ballroom floor, letting my tears fall from my eyes as I sob heavily.

“Ona-sama... what have I done?”

Just as I was about to lay down on the floor, a tall figure approached in my direction, wearing his regal outfit that somehow matches the same color as... mine? He slowly pulls me upwards, wiping the tears off my cheek. His gaze and touch felt subtle, until I slowly realized who he was.

“O- Ona... sama?”

As he opened his eyes, the ballroom lights began to lit up like the morning daylight. His soft, supple skin just happened to make me feel warm. His charming voice that I missed throughout my life at the island began to bloom as he said the words:

“Remember the hood, remember me. Not just as the Owner of the prestigious Venus Island, but also as your Prince.”

I smile in awe, immediately wrapping him in an embrace, tears falling off my cheeks as I close my eyes. “Ona-sama... where have you been?”

“I just got winded up... You might as well call it an element of surprise,” he giggles softly, continuing to wipe my tears off.

I pout, looking at his beautiful face. “Ona-sama!”

He chuckles, holding my body close. “Let’s go back to the plaza... everyone is waiting for us there.”

“Yeah...” I shift away from the embrace, holding his hand as we head out of the ballroom and out of the palace. At the plaza, the King and Queen are waiting alongside Lobelia and the invited guests. The spectators began reveling as soon as we approached the red carpet.

A teary smile brought upon my face as I walked down the aisle with Ona-sama. It felt like heaven when I’m on his side.

As we make our way to the podium, I lead the salutations and a heartwarming message of hope and love to everyone, not to mention the amount of support that they gave to me during the times where I have to make the toughest decisions in life.

“In addition... I’m forever grateful that I met the sweetest person who will complete my whole life... the one that I could count on during my brightest days and darkest nights. Without him, I won’t be where I am right now. I’ll hand over the floor to him for his salutations.”

I turn back, gazing at him as I step out of the podium. “Ona-sama?”

As he slowly steps on the podium to give his salutations, I just can’t help but hold his hand out of the blue.

“Uh...” He chuckles, continuing his salutations. “I just want to thank everyone for welcoming me in advance just so that I can plan this surprise. Again, sorry about that, my lady.”

“It’s fine,” I chuckle back. “Please proceed.”

“Alright... I gotta say, this party, as everyone would know, is for someone who believes that the power of love can mean so much to us... can mean more than just heartfelt memories. And with this, I dedicate this toast...” he pauses, raising his wine glass. “... to the woman who will be forever grateful to become the most beautiful bride of the kingdom. The one whom I was called the dearest Prince. A toast... to our dearest Fiona, as she celebrates her birthday today.”

I raise my wineglass while holding his hand. Everyone does the same. Some raised their glass of water, others raised their soft drinks. A few raise their mug of morning coffee.

“Well then, let’s enjoy the whole day of her birthday party!” The King declared, encouraging everyone to participate as they revel in the celebration. I look at the King and Queen as they smile at me. Ona-sama and Lobelia are already on good terms, seemingly because both he and I admitted to her about our relationship.

“Tell me... When did you really come here?” I turn to gaze at him, waiting for his response.

“I gotta tell you, it was three days ago.”

“What? B- but how did you slip through without us knowing? You could’ve told me!” I pout.

He chuckles. “Ahaha... I got in touch with the tailor who made some distinct hoods so the guards can’t see me. Some residents tend me to their delicacies. I have to admit: It’s my first time around here that I have to familiarize myself with everything first before plotting the plan.”

“Aw, come on! Please don’t do that again, okay? I’ll be upset the next time you do that!”
