Everything Went Dark

Luna’s Inside Story

Published 19 November 2021

The blackout has put the whole operation into a standstill. Could they survive the lingering darkness with Luna’s help?


POV: Owner

It’s Saturday evening. I was about to finish my paperwork when I heard panic downstairs. Misaki and Sayuri raced upstairs as soon as I got out of the Suite. “Ona-san, we have a problem,” said Sayuri, appearing exhausted.

“What’s going on?” I ask.

“Alright, I just had a problem with one of our electrical systems and that needs to be fixed immediately,” Misaki said, handing over the note. it reads:

We got a problem with the electrical systems. Best to fix them immediately before that gets worse.

“This is bad,” I said, sighing. “I might not be the only one who can provide that expertise, though.”

“Could you think there’s another that might help you with that?” Sayuri asks, a little nervous.

“I don’t know, but I’ll see what I can do,” I said, patting their heads.

They both nod, returning to their rooms. I went back to the Suite, only to see the lights flicker around the room. “That’s bad.”

In some short milliseconds, the bulbs stopped lighting; and only the moonlight can be provided for the night. We just had a blackout.

Immediately using my flashlight, I begin my way out. Some girls got out of their rooms in fear of the dark, and they somehow found me there. “Ona-sama, what’s happening?!” Fiona asked in a nervous tone.

I softly pat her back, telling her to calm down. “Just take a deep breath and everything will be fine. I’ll explain everything when we’re outside.”

As soon as everyone is outside, I begin my remarks: “Alright ladies, we just had a blackout; alternatively, people call it a power outage. This is fairly uncommon since it’s our first time having this kind of problem. But if we work together just like what we did in the toughest situations, I’m sure we can alleviate that issue in no time at all.”

I clear my throat before proceeding to the next sentence. “We know that some of you have their expertise ranging from their capability to fight up to handling the machinery. So I think it’s a good idea to share those expertise to others so that everyone can participate. What do you say?”

They all nod at my sentiments.

“Very good,” I said. “So, I might need someone to help me with this issue and hopefully bring back the operations as fast as possible.”

Someone behind them is raising her hand as soon as I finish saying that sentence. “Can we have her in the front, please?”

Slowly, I begin to realize who that woman was as soon as she walks close. “Luna...”

She stops to gaze at my direction, still maintaining that same neutral look. By appearance, she wears an Alien Heart. “Yes, I tend to fix stuff sometimes.”

“Quite a curiosity. Okay, we need to help each other on this one. But first, we need to figure out if that can be fixed in no time at all.”

Immediately setting out to the back of the mansion, we enter the electrical room, which already has a lot of smoke. “What happened here?” I ask, coughing.

With the flashlight in hand, we begin to inspect the machinery. “Those were the old electrical stuff that still managed to run after several years,” said Luna. “The only major damage was that it got hit by weapons and debris when Omega raided this island.”

“Ah,” I realize. We could only fix some parts, but not everything because they got some major damages here and there, with the significance being the chaos. And besides, those were pretty old, too. We could invest in a new one of the same equivalent, but from what I heard a few years ago, those were very expensive. I’m not sure how the previous Owner managed to buy that one. Or maybe he didn’t, and this island was built WAY before he came here for the first time.

... maybe Zack did have these electronics before? It’s kinda evident by the fact that he had his own island (Zack Island) that he spent a whole lot of figures to get the electronics working.

As we inspected further, we noticed some leakages laying about on the floor. “Oil is used to power the island,” she said. “While there’s no smell when everything’s normal, there is when there’s a malfunction. And when it does, it smells horrible.”

“Damn. We need to get rid of this.”

“How do we do that?”
